Chicken Noodle Soup

This is a perfect recipe to make whether you need a pick me up when you're sick, or just want to enjoy a tasty meal.

You don't need a whole chicken to make homemade chicken noodle soup. Here's how how to make it with bone-in chicken, chewy egg noodles and an aromatics-infused broth.

Few things compare to a bowl of homemade chicken noodle soup. It's pure comfort food, espcially on sick days or cold nights. The best bowls feature tender chicken and vegetables, a flavorful broth, and chewy egg noodles. Here's how to make chicken noodle soup that captures all those elements.

Ingredients

  • 2-1/2 lbs bone-in chicken thighs
  • 1/2 Tsp salt
  • 1/2 Tsp pepper
  • 1/2 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 10 cups chicken broth
  • 4 celery ribs
  • 4 carrots
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 cups uncooked egg noodles
  • 1 Tbsp parsley
  • 1 Tbsp lemon joice

Instructions

  1. Pat chicken dry with paper towels; sprinkle with salt and pepper. In a stockpot, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add chicken in batches; cook until dark golden brown, 3-4 minutes. Remvoe chicken from pan; discard all but 2 tablespoons drippings.
  2. Add onion to drippings; cook and stir over medium-high heat until tender, 4-5 minutes. Add garlic; cook 1 minute longer. Add broth, stirring to loosen browned bits from pan. Bring to a boil. Return chicken to pan. Add celery, carros, bayleaves and thyme. Reduce heat; simmer, covered, until chicken is tender, 25-30 minutes.
  3. Transfer chicken to a plate. Remove soup from heat. Add noodles; let stand, covered, until noodles are tender, 20-22 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, when chicken is cool enought to handle, remove meat from bones; discard bones. Shred meat into bite-sized pieces. Return meat to stockpot. Stir in parsley and lemon juice. If desired, adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper. Discard bay leaves.
